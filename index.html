I am an assistant professor of computer science at Vali-e-Asr university of Rafsanjan, Iran. My research interests include Big data models and algorithms, post-quantum cryptosystems, approximate reasoning and scientific computing. Before joining the VRU as a faculty, I have spent 5 years and a half at Yazd university to obtain my PhD and MSc degrees and 4 years at Shahid Bahonar university of Kerman to do my BSc, all in computer science. Current Courses

Theory of Computation - BSc

This is a second course in computability theory for CS undergraduates. At first, we start our discussion with Turing machines and study (un)decidability in terms of TMs. Then, we continue our study with theory of recursion and give a taste of Church-Turing thesis. Finally, we discuss some basic results in complexity theory, especially topics in NP- and PASPACE-completeness. Read More

Fundamentals of Secure Computing - BSc

This is the first, and the only, exposure of CE undergraduates to the topic of cybersecurity. In this course, we start by discussing how to design and evaluate secure systems with a human centeric ficus. Then, we explore the foundations of software security by considering some of the most important software vulnerabilities, attacks and defences. Next, we will discuss the foundations of modern cryptography from a practical perspective. Finally, the course is concluded by a brief introduction to hardware security and physical attacks to digital systems. Read more

Computer Programming for Physics Students - BSc

In this course, we will learn about fundamentals of problem solving with computers in Fortran 95, for students of Physics. Read more

Theory of Computer Science - MSc

In this course, we will explore some advanced topics in theory of computability and reccursion. We also discuss some foundation topics in theory of complexity. For the second half of the course, we discuss fixed-parameter algorithms. Read more

Network Security - MSc

In this course, we will explore some advanced topics in information, computer and network security. The course starts with the study of cryptographic algorithms including symmetric, asymmetric cryptosystems and hash functions. Then, we will explore the applications of these building blocks in construction of several protocols such as mutual trust, network security and computer security. Read more

Data Mining - MSc

In this course, we will explore data mining techniques including clustering, classification and recommender systems for massive (Big) datasets. We will also study the stream data model and related problems. Read more

Advanced Algorithms Design - MSc

In this course, we will review some fundamental techniques in algorithm design, e.g. dynamic programming, divide & conquer, backtrack and greedy algorithms. Then, we will extend our study to approximation schemes, randomized algorithms, fixed-parameter tractability and exact algorithms. The course is mostly based on recent papers. Read more
